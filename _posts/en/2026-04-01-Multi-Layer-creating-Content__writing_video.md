# Multi Layer when creating Content {writing, video, ...}

I just realized — creating Content in the form of writing is not as easy as I imagined.<br/>
Even though everything I write on this website is written for myself {as mentioned in the article [Writing for 'Future of Me'](/en/Writing-for-Future-of-Me/)}, the fact remains:

> **Writing that is useful**
>
> - useful for my future self, in the "Days Ahead"
> - useful for Others {hopefully}

what if everything is written in a messy way?<br/>
Future of Me: will cry reading the writings on this Website.

😁😁😁
he he he…

I think, at the very least, writing goes through **multi-layers**:

- **Layer 1** — idea / content: "what do I want to write?"
- **Layer 2** — structure: "how to organize it so it's easy to understand?"
- **Layer 3** — reader's perspective: "is it clear enough for others?"
- **Layer 4** — technical: "does it render correctly on Jekyll / GitHub Pages?"

By the way, what is _Jekyll / GitHub Pages_ + its implementation on this Website, is explained in [this](/en/SoftwareDevelopeRx-com-GitHub-Pages-site-Jekyll/) article.

For the world of Video Content, it probably requires several more layers:

- **idea** — same as writing
- **script** — must sound natural when spoken, not just read
- **recording** — lighting, audio, background, expression
- **editing** — cuts, transitions, music, subtitles
- **thumbnail** — one image that must "win" among thousands of videos
- **title & description** — SEO, clickable, but not clickbait
- **publish timing** — when to upload for maximum traffic
- **respond to comments** — engagement
